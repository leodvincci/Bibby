## Part 1: What is an Opportunity Thesis?

### Definition

An **Opportunity Thesis** is a concise, evidence-based document that makes the case for why a specific business opportunity exists, why it's valuable, and why you're positioned to capture it.

**Think of it as:**
- **Investor pitch:** Why should someone fund this?
- **Strategic plan:** What's our path to market leadership?
- **North star:** What decision-making framework guides us?
- **Recruiting tool:** Why should top talent join us?

### The Six Core Questions

Every Opportunity Thesis must answer:

1. **Market:** What's the opportunity size? Who's the customer?
2. **Problem:** What pain are we solving? How acute is it?
3. **Solution:** What are we building? Why is it 10√ó better?
4. **Competitive Advantage:** Why will we win vs. incumbents?
5. **Economics:** How do we make money? What's the unit economics?
6. **Execution:** What's the roadmap? What could go wrong?

**Format:**
- **Length:** 1-2 pages written, 10-15 slides presented
- **Audience:** Investors, executives, strategic hires
- **Tone:** Confident but evidence-based, ambitious but realistic
- **Update frequency:** Quarterly (as you learn more)

### Why This Matters

**Without an Opportunity Thesis:**
- Random feature development (no north star)
- Inconsistent messaging (sales says X, marketing says Y)
- Strategic drift (pursuing every shiny opportunity)
- Can't raise capital (no clear investment thesis)

**With an Opportunity Thesis:**
- Clear prioritization (does this feature advance the thesis?)
- Aligned organization (everyone knows the "why")
- Focused execution (say no to distractions)
- Fundable business (investors see the vision)
